Scientific Methodology & Valuation Framework

I. Biomass & DBH Conversion

For field efficiency, circumference (C) is measured and converted to Diameter at Breast Height (d): $d = C / \pi$ Biomass is estimated using species regressions from Jenkins et al. (2003) where $dbh_{cm} = d \times 2.54$.

II. Sequestration & Economic Cost

Carbon (C) represents ~50% of dry biomass. CO2 Equivalent is derived using a molecular weight ratio of 3.6663. Valuation follows the U.S. EPA (2023) Social Cost of Carbon central estimate of $210 per metric ton.

Forestry: Jenkins, J. C., et al. (2003). National-scale biomass estimators.

Economics: U.S. EPA. (2023). Report on the Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases.
