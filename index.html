Scientific Methodology & Citations

I. Annual Increment Modeling

Calculations utilize total above-ground biomass (AGB) equations from Jenkins, J.C., et al. (2003). Unlike static storage models, this registry calculates the Current Annual Increment (CAI). This represents the additional carbon sequestered in the coming year as the tree increases in volume.

We assume a mean radial growth of 0.2 inches per year, which is then adjusted by the Visual Health Factor selected during the survey (Excellent to Dead).

II. Carbon & Economic Indexing

Molecular Ratio: Biomass is estimated at 50% dry-weight carbon. To convert to CO2 equivalent (CO2e), we apply the 3.6663 atomic weight ratio ($44/12$).

Economic Valuation: Social value is calculated via the EPA Social Cost of Carbon (2024 update), indexed at approximately $190 per metric ton, or $0.09 per pound of CO2e sequestered.
