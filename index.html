Scientific Methodology & Valuation Framework

I. Biomass & Condition Factor

Base biomass ($B$) is calculated via Jenkins et al. (2003). Storage capacity is adjusted by the Condition Factor ($C_f$), where a lower vitality score reduces the projected sequestered carbon asset.

Adjusted Carbon = $B \times 0.5 \times 3.6663 \times C_f$

II. Economic Social Cost

Valuation follows the U.S. EPA (2023) central estimate for the Social Cost of Carbon (SCC) at a 2% discount rate, valued at $190 per metric ton ($0.086 per lb). This represents the global economic damage prevented by the storage of this carbon.

Forestry: Jenkins, J. C., et al. (2003). Biomass Regressions for North American Tree Species.

Economics: U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (2023). Report on the Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases.
