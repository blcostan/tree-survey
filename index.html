Technical Methodology & Scientific Basis

I. Biomass Estimation (Jenkins Model)

This system utilizes National-scale biomass estimators developed specifically for US tree species. Unlike simplified volume-to-weight conversions, the Jenkins model accounts for variations in wood density and specific gravity across species groups.

Equation: bm = exp(b0 + b1 * ln(dbh))
Conversion: CO2e = (bm * 0.5) * 3.6663

The ratio 3.6663 reflects the molecular weight of CO2 (44) relative to Carbon (12).

II. Social Cost of Carbon (EPA SCC)

The Social Cost of Carbon (SCC) is a metric used to quantify the economic damages of climate change. It represents the value of damages avoided for every metric ton of CO2 sequestered or kept out of the atmosphere.

Following the EPA 2023 Supplementary Material, we apply a 2026 central estimate of $210/metric ton. This value accounts for net impacts on agricultural productivity, human health, property damage from increased flood risk, and ecosystem services.
