Scientific Methodology & Valuation Framework

I. Biomass & Condition Factor

Base biomass ($B$) is calculated via Jenkins et al. (2003). However, storage capacity and capital value are adjusted by the Condition Factor ($C_f$). This represents the tree's health and structural integrity; a tree in "Poor" condition has a reduced capacity for sequestration and a higher risk of carbon release through decay.

Adjusted Carbon = $B \times 0.5 \times 3.6663 \times C_f$

II. Sequestration & Economic Cost

CO2 Equivalent molecular ratio is 3.6663. Valuation follows the U.S. EPA (2023) Social Cost of Carbon central estimate of $190 per metric ton ($0.086 per lb).

Forestry: Jenkins, J. C., et al. (2003). Comprehensive Database of Diameter-based Biomass Regressions for North American Tree Species. USDA Forest Service.

Economics: U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (2023). Supplementary Material for the Social Cost of Greenhouse Gases.
