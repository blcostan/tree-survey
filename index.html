Scientific Methodology & Carbon Logic

I. The Biomass Equation

We use the Jenkins Allometric Power Law to estimate total tree weight based on Diameter at Breast Height (DBH).

Mass = exp(Constant_A + Constant_B * ln(DBH))

II. Carbon Fraction

Approximately 50% of a tree's dry mass is pure Carbon, physically sequestered from the atmosphere.

III. Atmosphere to Wood

To find total $CO_2$ removed, we multiply the Carbon weight by 3.666, representing the molecular ratio of $CO_2$ to $C$.

Final Formula Logic:

[(Biomass × 0.5) × 3.666] × Health Modifier = Total $CO_2$ Saved

Species Classification Guide

🌳 Hardwood (Sturdy)

Dense, slow-growing structural trees with broad leaves. Highly valued for long-term canopy architecture.

Includes: White Oak, Willow Oak, Southern Red Oak, Shagbark Hickory, Walnut, and American Beech.

🍃 Soft Hardwood (Fast Growth)

Deciduous trees with rapid cellular expansion patterns and lower fiber density levels.

Includes: Red Maple, Sugar Maple, Tulip Poplar, Sweetgum, Sycamore, and River Birch.

🌲 Evergreen (Conifers)

Needle-bearing specimens providing year-round environmental windbreak performance and shelter profiles.

Includes: Loblolly Pine, Longleaf Pine, Eastern Redcedar, and Deodar Cedar.

🌸 Small / Ornate

Understory species generally restricted to a lower maximum height, selected for aesthetic features.

Includes: Flowering Dogwood, Redbud, American Holly, Yoshino Cherry, and Crape Myrtle.

Tree Health Evaluation Guide

Evaluate the tree's complete structural state using the following standard forestry baselines:

⭐ Excellent (1.0)

Full, robust canopy with ideal tip growth extension. No visible structural decay, trunk wounds, pest damage, or dead wood components within upper crown sections.

🟢 Good (0.8)

Minor structural flaws or isolated dead branch tips present. Canopy displays strong foliage color and density distribution (>80% functional leafy surface area).

🟡 Fair (0.6)

Moderate localized branch dieback or minor trunk hollows observed. Obvious signs of insect pressure or environmental stress, but core vigor remains stable.

🟠 Poor (0.4)

Severe operational decay or extensive skeletal dieback across the main structure (>50% canopy loss). Major bark peeling, mechanical fracturing, or foundational root damage.

💀 Dead (0.25)

No living green tissue or functional leaf arrays remaining. Structural elements represent a complete biomass loss profile, retained primarily for specialized wildlife habitat values.
