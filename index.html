Scientific Methodology & Citations

I. Biomass Regression Models

Biomass estimation is calculated using generalized parameters from Jenkins, J.C. et al. (2003). The model follows the logarithmic power law: $$BM = Exp(b0 + b1 \times ln(DBH_{cm}))$$.

Adjustments are made for tree condition (multiplier 0.8) to reflect the estimated health-related biomass degradation in urban canopy specimens.

II. Carbon Sequestration & Economic Valuation

Dry biomass is converted to carbon (0.50) and subsequently to sequestered CO2 equivalents using the molecular weight ratio of 3.6663.

Economic valuation is indexed to the U.S. EPA (2023) Social Cost of Carbon (SCC). A localized rate of approximately $0.09/lb ($200/metric ton) is applied to represent long-term climate mitigation value.

Source: Forest Science 49(1):12-35; EPA-450-R-23-001
